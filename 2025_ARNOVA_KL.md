## Network leadership {#slide-4}

Cross-sector collaboration studies highlight  tha  leading organizations play a critical role in developing strategic plans and shaping network culture and performance.

Ansell & Gash (2007) highlight the importance of face-to-face dialogue, trust building, and the development of commitment and shared understanding for successful collaboration. 

Network leadership (nonprofit- versus government-led) may shape collaborative networks' strategies and operations.

Information sharing within collaborative networks (Valero et al., 2021)

Community orientation and relationship (Salamon, 1987; Young, 1998; Yang & Callahan, 2007)

Federal funding acquisition (Valero et al., 2021)

Nonprofit-led networks may be better positioned to identify community needs because they work more closely with the population they serve and maintain longer-term relationships with them (Van Slyke, 2006; Romzek et al. 2014).

## Policy Responsiveness -- Anti-camping Laws {#slide-6}

- Anti-camping laws are local ordinances that prohibit activities such as sleeping, camping, or storing personal belongings in public spaces. 

• 72% have at least one law restricting camping in public

• 51% have at least one law prohibiting sleeping in public

• 55% have at least one law banning sitting and lying down in public

- A survey of 351 people experiencing homelessness (PEH) living on the streets and in public parks in San   Francisco found that 90% were forced to move along at least once in the previous year; 84% received a citation (Herring et al., 2020).
- 
- Local criminalization ordinances cannot be relied upon to reduce the number of PEH and therefore do not provide the intended community-level benefit (Lebovits & Sullivan, 2025).

## Narratives: How to gather feedback from PLE {#slide-15}

How your CoC gathers feedback from people experiencing homelessness?

FL507, Led by nonprofit organization, Homeless Service Network.

CoC routinely gathers feedback from persons w. lived experienced ( PwLE ), formally via active  Lived Experience Council (LEC)  &  Youth Action Board (YAB), Race/Equity Data Workgroup, & on all CoC w representation of  PwLE  as members; & informally as CoC membership has rich & imbedded lived expertise.  An informal survey of 56 CoC members in 2023 indicated over 50% (33/56) have LE. 

## Narratives: How to gather feedback from PLE {#slide-16}

how often your CoC gathers feedback from people experiencing homelessness?

Feedback from  PwLE  is regular & ongoing through boards, committees, workgroups that gather at least monthly, informal feedback  that is incorporated in daily work, & intentionally solicited for use in formal annual planning, & in the development of new initiatives, like low-barrier shelter , YHDP & YHSI programs.

## Slide 17

Findings

CoC networks with greater involvement from people with lived experience tend to report higher levels of implementation of anti-criminalization measures.

Nonprofit-led networks report lower levels of anti-criminalization strategy implementation relative to government-led networks.

Conclusion

Network leadership and their engagement with PLE may shape networks' attitudes and strategies toward law enforcement responses that criminalize PEH.
