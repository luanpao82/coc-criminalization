How Grants Pass v. Johnson becomes an identification strategy: two competing hypotheses, a multilevel decomposition of political environment, and an honest account of the DV's measurement-invariance issue.

Theory — two competing hypotheses

H1 · Organizational form

Nonprofit-led CoCs — more insulated from electoral pressure and with closer ties to service providers and advocates — should respond to a federal legal shock enabling criminalization (Grants Pass) by raising their anti-criminalization posture relative to government-led CoCs.

H2 · Local political environment

CoCs embedded in Democratic-leaning political environments — whether at the state level (H2a) or in terms of county-within-state deviation (H2b) — should raise their anti-criminalization posture relative to Republican-leaning environments. Governance structure is irrelevant in this story; politics filters the shock.

Multilevel decomposition of political environment

Because CoCs are nested (CoCs ⊂ counties ⊂ states), and because state and county politics are highly correlated, we decompose county Biden vote share into two orthogonal components:

county_biden_share = state_mean_share      ← H2a (between-state)
                   + biden_within_state    ← H2b (within-state)

The between-state component is summarized as a binary blue_state indicator (Biden won the state's EC vote in 2020). The within-state component is continuous and mean-zero by construction. Both enter the regression together without collinearity.

  • Estimator: Papke-Wooldridge fractional logit (bounded [0, 1] DV with mass at 0 and 1).
  • Standard errors: state-level cluster-robust, reinforced with wild-cluster bootstrap (Rademacher weights, 999 replicates). Addresses small-cluster over-rejection per Bertrand, Duflo & Mullainathan (2004).
  • Sample: unbalanced panel (651 obs) for primary power; balanced three-year panel (375 obs) reported as sensitivity.
  • Mundlak means: adding CoC-level means of time-varying controls preserves identification of the DiD coefficients without absorbing the time-invariant IVs, which within-CoC fixed effects would otherwise do.

DV measurement invariance — the load-bearing caveat

The outcome comes from HUD's 1D-4 question, which was reworded in FY2024:

ColumnFY2022 / FY2023 wordingFY2024 wording
1 · Policymaker engagement "Engaged local policymakers to prevent criminalization" "Engaged legislators on co-responder responses"
2 · Implementation "Reverse existing criminalization policies" "Implemented Laws/Policies/Practices that Prevent Criminalization"

The column-2 reword is mechanically easier to check Yes — any existing policy qualifies, not just a reversal. Descriptives show the FY2024 jump is concentrated in column 2, consistent with at least some of the level shift being an instrument effect.

Three design choices handle this:

  1. Year fixed effect (the post indicator) absorbs the common instrument-change shift.
  2. DiD interactions identify differential response — the instrument change cancels out of any contrast between CoCs facing the same reworded form.
  3. Engagement-only DV (results.html robustness) restricts to the column-1 cells whose wording was nearly identical across years.
